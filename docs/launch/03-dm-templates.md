# DM + outreach templates

The single rule: **be asymmetric.** Offer first, ask never (or last). Make every DM something a real human writing in their voice would send.

---

## A. Substack Recommendations cold-DM

**When**: Days 12-13 (May 21-22), AFTER 7-10 days of restacks + comments.

**Hard rule**: do NOT send this until you've added the target's publication to YOUR Recommendations first. The DM is "I already did the thing you'd be doing for me, no ask, here's a heads-up."

**Targets and warmth ranking (warmest → coldest):**

1. **Concoda** (Conor) — methodologically-aligned, indie-respecting, has linked open-source tools before
2. **Macro Notes** (Aleksandar) — analogue-framing-friendly per current essays
3. **fx:macro** (Marc Roth) — tactical, FX-tilted, smaller subscriber base = higher recip rate
4. **Capital Flows Research** (Cole Tunder) — quant-rigour audience, OTI fits
5. **Stay-At-Home Macro** (Joey Politano-adjacent / Nathan Tankus) — long-form, methodology-respecting
6. **Apricitas** (Joey Politano) — biggest fish, hardest sell, save for second-round
7. **FXMacroGuy** — fast tactical FX, quick DM-response cadence

**Template (subbed for Concoda; verbatim works):**

```
Subject: 1929 + 2008 in your data, OTI's analogue engine, and the May
2026 setup

Hi Conor —

Long-time reader. I run OTI (oti.dev), an open-source historical-macro-
analogue engine. Just published a piece on the 1973-vs-1998 read of the
current Treasury curve that's getting some HN traction.

Wanted to flag two things:

1. I've added Concoda to OTI's Substack Recommendations today. No ask.
   Your Apr 28 piece on Fed-balance-sheet-as-policy-channel is exactly
   the gap OTI was built for — that's the kind of analytical
   trajectory we now store structurally on every event in the corpus.

2. The OTI MCP server (Claude Desktop / Cursor) exposes the corpus +
   methodology as Resources — your readers can pin oti://corpus/
   manifest.json into a Claude session and ask "what does today rhyme
   with" inside their existing workflow. If that's interesting to your
   audience, happy to write up a guest post on the Concoda Substack
   walking through it (you can publish or not, your call).

OTI is MIT, no monetisation, just trying to externalise the historical-
analogue mental library smart macro PMs already keep privately.

Repo: github.com/Al033/OTI · Live: oti.dev

— Alan
```

**Why this works:**
- Specific reference to a real Concoda post (proves you read)
- "Added to recommendations today" — done deal, no reciprocity ask
- Concrete offer (guest post writeup) framed as opt-in for them
- Closing reinforces no-money posture

**What NOT to do:**
- Generic "love your work" opener
- "Would love to collaborate" closer
- Asking them to recommend OTI back (let them volunteer; if they don't, the recommendation cost you nothing)
- Writing 200+ words

**Variants per target**: identical template, swap in (a) the target's name, (b) one specific recent essay you genuinely read, (c) the OTI feature most relevant to their audience.

## B. Pre-brief for newsletter writers (Day 12, ahead of essay #3)

**When**: Wed May 21 09:00 ET, 48h before the Friday May 23 essay #3 publish.

**Goal**: 3 macro newsletter writers know the essay is coming, get the headline + chart 48h before public, can prep their own commentary.

**Targets**: Concoda + Macro Notes + Capital Flows Research (the 3 most likely to engage on quant-methodology essays).

**Template:**

```
Subject: 48h heads-up — OTI essay #3 dropping Friday 09:00 ET

Hi [name],

Heads-up: I'm publishing my third OTI essay this Friday at 09:00 ET,
title "5 things our analogue model is screaming about June 2026 that
consensus is missing."

If you'd like, I can send you the draft + the chart pack 24h ahead of
publish — your read is welcome as commentary, embedded in the essay
or in your own follow-up. No expectation either way.

Pinging because [SPECIFIC RECENT TAKE OF THEIRS] would slot directly
into the [SPECIFIC ESSAY POINT] I'm making.

Thread it / fade it / ignore it — completely your call.

— Alan
```

**Why 48h not 24h**: lets them think about it without time pressure. 24h is too rushed for a subscriber-funded writer; 48h gives them an option to publish their own piece as a reaction.

**When**: First pitch Day 21 (Fri May 30), follow-up if no response Day 30.

**Targets**: Forward Guidance (Jack Farley), Macro Voices (Erik Townsend), Odd Lots stringer (Joe Weisenthal / Tracy Alloway), Acquired ACQ2 (Ben Gilbert / David Rosenthal — for the open-source-finance angle).

**Template:**

```
Subject: Ep idea — historical macro analogues, AI, and why "memory not
prediction" is the right finance-LLM frame

Hi [host name],

Pitching an episode for [show name].

I built OTI (oti.dev), open-source, MIT, historical-macro-analogue
engine. Type "Trump tariffs 2025" → get 1971 Nixon shock + 2018 Powell

The framing — and the reason your audience would care — is that almost
every "AI in finance" project pretends to predict and they all fail
the audience. OTI is the contrarian play: pattern-recognition over

What I could bring to a 60-min episode:
  - Live demo of the engine running against any current setup the
    audience submits
  - The "memory not prediction" framing argument — why every other
    finance-LLM project is downstream of getting this wrong
  - The HN launch metrics (we had a Show HN front page on May 13)
  - The community-corpus contribution flow as a moat-building
    pattern other indie finance tools could steal
```

**Why this works:**
- Specific to the show's audience (your audience would care because…)
- Concrete "what I'd bring" rather than vague ("I'd be a great guest")
- Pre-emptively offers the work (sample audio, ramp doc) without making it a hard ask
- Numbers if you have them (HN launch traction)

## D. Reply-to-comment template (for HN, Reddit, X)

If a comment is critical and asking a real question:

```
Author here, thanks for the push.

[Restate the critique in your own words to show you understood.]

Two responses:

  1. [Where I think the critique is right and what we're doing about it.]
  2. [Where I'd respectfully push back and the specific reasoning.]

If [specific actionable thing], I'd genuinely be interested in your
take.

— Alan, oti.dev
```

If a comment is unfair or trollish:

```
Author here. [Single-sentence rebuttal of the inaccuracy, no more.]

Repo: github.com/Al033/OTI — happy to discuss specifics over there.
```

**Don't engage trolls beyond a single-line factual correction. Your time on Day 4 is more valuable spent replying to substantive critics.**
